To show distributivity, we must consider two types. For the first, let $z \in \mathbb{C}=F$ and $v_1, v_2 \in \mathbb{C}=V$. Then, $z \star  (v_1 + v_2) = \overline{z} (v_1 + v_2)$. This can be rewritten as $\overline{z}v_1 + \overline{z}v_2 = z \star v_1 + z \star v_2$. For the second kind of distributivity, now let $z_1, z_2 \in \mathbb{c}=F$ and $v \in \mathbb{C}=V$. We can write the term $(z_1 + z_2) \star v = (\overline{z_1 + z_2})v$.
